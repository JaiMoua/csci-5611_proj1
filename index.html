Pinball Simulation Project Report

The Pinball Simulation project is a digital replica of the vintage pinball arcade game. This concept replicates the thrill and dynamics of classic pinball by utilizing cutting-edge physics calculations and interactive visuals. To get points, players may manipulate flippers, launch the ball with an animated plunger, and interact with a variety of barriers. The simulation provides a true pinball game experience with enhanced bright images and engrossing sound effects.

Project Scope

Our project's main objective is to explain:

  • Conceptualizing and designing the game/simulation mechanics.
  • Developing and implementing core physic.
  • Creating high-quality graphics, animations, and audio assets.

Features Implemented

We attempted several features in our simulation/game. Here is a list of the key features, along with a brief description to the corresponding sections in the video(s).

Realistic Physics

Our simulation/game features a physics engine that accurately simulates the behavior of objects and people to increase realism. Players are submerged in a plausible gaming environment by this feature, which includes lifelike motions and realistic collisions.

Loading Multiple Scenes

We have designed and demonstrated at least two distinct scenes/table arrangements within our simulation/game to highlight the flexibility of this functionality. We increase replayability and make sure that players may have a wide variety of pinball experiences by including this scene loading functionality.

Score Display

At the heart of our score display system is a real-time score counter. Players' scores are regularly updated and visibly shown on the screen as they engage with the game and complete various goals. This gives players the ability to monitor their progress and compete for high scores, which gives the gaming a competitive and satisfying element.

Sound Effect

The sound effect mostly illustrates how the ball behaves when it collides with walls or  circle objects. Additionally, sound display is applied to both the plunger and the flipper to make user experiences more lifelike.

Difficulties Encountered

We give a thorough summary of the difficulties and roadblocks we had while creating our simulation/game in this part. It's essential to present these difficulties in a structured and informative manner, highlighting both the nature of the challenges and the solutions we devised to overcome them.

Lack of resources

A noteworthy absence of internet information for a key component was one of the biggest challenges we encountered while working on our project. The deployment of a crucial feature was difficult because of the scarcity of easily available information. We conducted course research, interacted with office hours for feedback, and, as appropriate, created specialized solutions to address this issue. We were able to successfully solve this knowledge gap because to our multifaceted strategy.

Transmitting Code

The requirement to communicate our project's sizable codebase without depending on conventional version control systems like GitHub was one of the distinctive problems we encountered in this project. This problem developed since the Processing app does not have a built-in terminal, which restricted our ability to access public code hosting services. We used a mix of different approaches to get over this problem. To enable code sharing among team members, we set up a discord server.

Time management

Throughout our project, effective time management arose as a recurring difficulty. It was challenging to balance several development-related responsibilities while meeting deadlines due to conflict hours. We put in place a structured project plan with clearly defined milestones, ranked activities according to dependencies, met often as a team to monitor progress, and used time-tracking software. We were able to reduce our processes and keep the project on track thanks to these tactics.
